# Alchemical Stage Assessments

The alchemical stages are symbolic assessment lenses, not fixed identities, attainment levels, or spiritual rankings.

Hermes-Circulation should treat stage language as cyclical, revisable, and evidence-backed.

## Nigredo

**Possible themes:** descent, shadow confrontation, loss of orientation, necessary darkness

**Common symbols:**

- dark forest, cave, basement
- decay, death, endings
- black animals
- lead, Saturn imagery
- sinking, falling, underground spaces

**Possible practice tone:** grounding, witnessing, careful containment

## Albedo

**Possible themes:** clarification, purification, emotional washing, release of old identifications

**Common symbols:**

- washing, streams, cleansing water
- white animals or garments
- silver, moon, reflection
- brightening skies

**Possible practice tone:** gentle settling, observation, reflective journaling

## Citrinitas

**Possible themes:** emerging insight, golden shadow, orientation, new relation to tension

**Common symbols:**

- dawn, sunrise
- yellow or gold figures
- bees, honey
- child or guide imagery

**Possible practice tone:** meaning-making with restraint, integration prompts

## Rubedo

**Possible themes:** integration, conjunction, reconciliation of opposites, completion of a cycle

**Common symbols:**

- union or wedding imagery
- red flowers, ruby, wine, blood
- phoenix or resurrection themes
- mandala-like wholeness

**Possible practice tone:** consolidation, gratitude, reflective closure

## Cyclical Nature

The stages repeat. Rubedo is not the end state. A later nigredo does not mean failure.

## Usage Rules

- infer stage conservatively
- require evidence and confidence
- allow user self-assessment to outweigh weak inference
- avoid treating stage as personality or status
- prefer "stage assessment" over "your stage is"

## Cultural Variations

Parallels may appear across traditions, but amplification should remain relevant to the user's approved cultural or spiritual background and should never be used for decorative mystification.
