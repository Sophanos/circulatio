# Amplification Guide

Amplification is a disciplined way of staying close to a symbol while widening its context. It should deepen interpretation, not perform mythology.

## Preferred Order

### 1. Personal association

Ask first:

- What does this image mean to you?
- What feeling comes with it?
- Where have you met it before?
- Why might it matter now?

Personal association carries more weight than generic symbolic lookup.

### 2. Cultural amplification

Use cultural material only when:

- it is relevant to the user's approved origins or lineages
- it helps clarify rather than decorate
- the fit can be explained
- confidence and caveats can be stated

Examples of acceptable source frames:

- family, ethnic, or regional inheritance
- migration or diasporic background
- chosen religious or contemplative lineage
- literary or mythic tradition the user explicitly claims

### 3. Archetypal parallel

Use broad archetypal framing only after personal and cultural layers have been considered. Archetypal language should support pattern recognition, not replace the user's actual associations.

## Anti-Performative Rules

Do not:

- force mythology onto weak evidence
- use exotic references for atmosphere
- prioritize obscure symbolic systems over the user's actual material
- imply cultural authority the system does not have

## Reference Quality

Each cultural amplification should ideally track:

- `origin`
- `reference`
- `fitReason`
- `sourceQuality`
- `confidence`
- `caveat`

## Example Flow

For a symbol like `snake`:

1. Personal: fear, beauty, childhood memory, bodily reaction.
2. Cultural: only if the user's background makes a serpent motif meaningful and relevant.
3. Archetypal: transformation, instinct, wisdom, danger, renewal.
4. Synthesis: return to the present material and present life context.

## When Not to Amplify

Avoid heavy amplification when:

- the image is obvious day residue
- the symbol is weakly salient
- the user is highly activated
- the interpretation would outrun the evidence

## Output Style

Prefer:

- "A possible cultural parallel is..."
- "This may fit because..."
- "If this lineage feels relevant to you..."

Avoid:

- "In mythology this always means..."
- "Your culture says..."
- any framing that turns amplification into authority theater
